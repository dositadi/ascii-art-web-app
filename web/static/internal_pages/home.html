ASCII standard published
The American Standards Association publishes ASCII as a 7-bit character encoding, enabling interoperability between electronic devices for the first time.

1970s
ASCII art emerges
Early programmers begin assembling characters into pictures and banners on dot-matrix printers and CRT terminals, giving birth to ASCII art as a creative form.

Bulletin board systems spread ASCII art globally. Groups like ACiD Productions and iCE Advertisements elevate it to a respected digital art form.
